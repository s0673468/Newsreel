## APOE4 & Neurodegeneration

**Nature Aging: APOE4 drives early hippocampal neuron hyperactivity + shrinkage via Nell2 — years before memory loss**
Gladstone Institute team shows APOE4 (vs APOE3) upregulates the secreted protein **Nell2** in hippocampal neurons. Nell2 causes dendritic shrinkage and chronic hyperexcitability in the same hippocampal circuits that fail first in AD — in young APOE4 animals, before any amyloid or tau pathology. Genetic knockdown of Nell2 *rescues* both the morphology and the firing rate. The pathway: APOE4 → aberrant lipid handling → Nell2 transcription up → dendritic retraction + excitability → decades of metabolic strain → clinical decline. This is a new mechanistic handle that sits *upstream* of amyloid.
[Medical Xpress](https://medicalxpress.com/news/2026-04-alzheimer-gene-disrupts-brain-circuits.html) | [Psychologs writeup](https://www.psychologs.com/new-research-reveals-how-apoe4-causes-brain-cell-shrinkage-years-before-alzheimers/)

> **Personal relevance (high — direct):** This is the most important APOE4 finding of the week. The "hyperactivity-then-atrophy" phenotype lines up with why early-life interventions matter so much in ε4/ε4 — you're fighting a *circuit-level* drain that's running silently from your 30s. Three practical implications: (1) anything that dampens excitotoxic glutamate signaling (magnesium L-threonate, taurine, moderate exercise-induced GABA) now has a mechanistic "why" beyond sleep. (2) Chronic BDNF support (ashwagandha, exercise, citicoline) is the direct counterweight to dendritic retraction. (3) Nell2 inhibition is a plausible future APOE4-specific drug target — worth tracking which labs pick it up.

**Proportion of AD attributable to APOE — new npj Dementia analysis: ~70% of cases**

## LLM Architecture & AI Research

**Sparser, Faster, Lighter Transformers (arXiv Mar 2026) — L1 regularization hits >99% FFN sparsity with no quality loss, 20% faster train and inference**
Team shows that a mild L1 penalty on Transformer++ FFN activations (the architecture shared by Qwen, Llama, Gemma) drives sparsity past 99% with negligible downstream degradation. Practical wins: up to **20.5% faster forward pass** and **21.9% faster training** on billion-parameter models. The "almost free" nature is the interesting part — you're not quantizing, not distilling, not changing attention; you're just letting the network discover which gated FFN units matter and throwing the rest away at compile time.
[arXiv PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2603.23198)

> **Systems lens:** This is the same insight as unused-branch elimination in a compiler — once the network has self-identified dead code, you can prune without behavioral change. For local models on a 5090, this kind of post-training sparsity is *the* path to squeezing a Qwen-72B-class model into 24 GB VRAM without accuracy cliff. Expect Ollama/llama.cpp forks within a month.
