## LLM Architecture & Training

### Path-Constrained MoE Tries to Turn Expert Routing Into an Actual Search Process
Instead of one-shot top-k routing, PathMoE lets the router explore a learned path across experts, lifting performance by 12.8% on average and by as much as 42.9% on complex reasoning tasks while staying parameter-efficient. The useful abstraction is tree search in expert space: routing becomes multi-step credit assignment rather than a single noisy gate. If that result holds, MoE gains may come less from bigger expert pools and more from better traversal policies.

### Hybrid Associative Memories Aim at the "Long Context Without Quadratic Tax" Problem
This architecture combines exact local attention with a global associative memory, letting linear-attention models recover exact-attention-style recall while claiming up to 40x faster inference and 10x lower VRAM at 128k context. The interesting part is not the speedup headline; it is the split-memory design. Short-range detail stays precise, long-range retrieval gets compressed into a separate store, which is closer to a cache hierarchy than the monolithic memory model standard transformers still use.

## AI Agents & RL

### Agentic Critical Training Replaces Binary Success Labels With Difficulty-Aware Signals
ACT scores every step along a continuous difficulty axis and uses that richer signal to train agents, outperforming outcome-only RL baselines by 6-10% on four benchmarks. This matters because binary pass/fail telemetry is information-starved; it throws away where the agent nearly had the problem solved. More dense supervision is the same trick that works in systems monitoring: better observability beats louder hope.

[Source](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.08706)

### Strategy-Guided Exploration Teaches Agents Reusable Semantic Policies, Not Just Trajectories
SGE trains agents to discover and reuse high-level strategies during exploration, improving online task performance by 18.3 percentage points across seven benchmarks. The key idea is separation of concerns: learn portable "ways of approaching tasks" first, then fill in action-level details later. That feels closer to how humans transfer skill across tools and websites.
