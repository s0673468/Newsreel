## APOE4 & Neurodegeneration

### Lithium Deficiency Directly Causes Alzheimer's Pathology in Mice
Reducing endogenous cortical lithium by ~50% markedly increased amyloid-β deposition, phospho-tau accumulation, pro-inflammatory microglial activation, synapse/axon/myelin loss, and accelerated cognitive decline. Replacement therapy with lithium orotate — a salt with reduced amyloid binding — prevented all pathological changes and memory loss in both AD model and aging wild-type mice. Mechanism confirmed via GSK-3β activation.

**Personal relevance:** Direct validation of your lithium orotate protocol. The Nature paper positions lithium not as a therapeutic add-on but as a potential deficiency state — think of it like vitamin D: you're not supplementing, you're correcting a deficit.

[Source](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-025-09335-x)

### APOE4→APOE2 Gene Therapy Reverses Pathology in Mice
Astrocyte-specific replacement of APOE4 with protective APOE2 improved cognition, decreased amyloid pathology, lowered gliosis, and normalized metabolic and gene expression signatures. This is the strongest preclinical signal yet that APOE4's damage is not destiny — it's a modifiable variable if you can swap the protein.

**Personal relevance:** Not actionable today, but this is the gene therapy pipeline to watch. Think of it as the "rewrite the buggy library" approach vs. your current "add middleware to compensate" strategy.

[Source](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41593-025-02094-y)

## LLM Architecture & Training

### Grokking Confirmed in Practical LLM Pretraining
First demonstration that grokking — the delayed memorization→generalization phase transition — occurs during real LLM pretraining, not just toy problems. Using MoE models, researchers tracked expert routing patterns evolving from "random, instance-specific" to "structured, transferable across samples." Different data subgroups transition asynchronously. Zero-cost monitoring metrics developed via expert routing consistency — no test set needed.

**Why it matters:** Reframes grokking from "curious phenomenon" to "fundamental training dynamic." If you can detect the memorization→generalization boundary cheaply, you get better decisions on training schedules, data mixing, and early stopping. Observability for a previously black-box process.

[Source](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.21551)

---

### Provable Scaling Laws for Feature Emergence via Grokking
Li² framework proposes three grokking stages: (I) Lazy learning, (II) independent feature learning, (III) interactive feature learning — with provable scaling laws connecting phases to feature emergence. Validates on modern optimizers including Muon.

[Source](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.21519v3)

### ICLR 2026: Transformers Are Rarely a Local Optimum
Replaces GeLUs and softmax with parametrized splines optimized on held-out data. Finding: standard transformers are rarely a local optimum in architecture space — simple alternatives can perform significantly better but sacrifice universality.

## Sleep & Circadian Biology

### Circadian Regularity Beats Sleep Duration as Health Predictor
Inconsistent circadian regularity — independent of sleep duration — predicts adverse health outcomes, diminished performance, mood decline, and depression risk. ## Exercise Science

### Zone 2 Hype Challenged: Higher Intensities More Efficient
Narrative review (Storoschuk et al., 2025) argues current evidence does not support Zone 2 as the optimal intensity for mitochondrial or fatty acid oxidative capacity in the general population. Higher intensities consistently produce greater improvements. The Zone 2 gospel stems from elite athletes doing 15-20+ hours/week — doesn't translate to <6 hours/week.

**Personal relevance:** With BJJ, surfing, kickboxing, and lifting already in your schedule, your Zone 2 time (≥120 min/week) may be better spent at slightly higher intensities. If you're CPU-bound on training hours, higher-intensity work has better ROI per minute. [Source](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40560504/)
