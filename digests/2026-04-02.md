## APOE4 & Neurodegeneration

### Blood-Based APOE4 Zygosity Testing Is Getting Close to Commodity Infrastructure
A newly indexed plasma immunoassay classified APOE epsilon4 zygosity with 99.6% concordance to PCR-based genotyping, including 100.0% concordance in participants with Alzheimer's disease. The important shift is operational, not conceptual: APOE-aware screening and treatment routing still depend on genetics infrastructure many clinics do not have. If this result holds up in routine practice, genotype status moves closer to a standard lab primitive instead of a specialty bottleneck.

## AI Agents & RL

### Agent Security Is Finally Moving From "Behave Better" to Least-Privilege Design
The AC4A paper argues current agents are over-trusted because tool access is still mostly all-or-nothing, then proposes Unix-like permissions across APIs and web pages. That is the right abstraction. Classical systems stopped scaling the moment everything ran as root, and agent frameworks are rediscovering the same lesson. If this approach lands, agent capability will increasingly be shaped by permission architecture, not just better prompting.

### Telmisartan Still Looks Like the Most Brain-Interesting ARB in the Class
A recent hypertension cohort found telmisartan had the lowest risk of all-cause dementia among the compared RAS inhibitors, with hazard ratios of 0.78 versus non-telmisartan ARBs and 0.67 versus ACE inhibitors; the paper also reports lower stroke risk and still describes the effect as marginal. This is observational, so it is not permission to tell a clean causal story. But it does keep telmisartan on the short list of blood-pressure drugs that may be doing more than cuff control, especially if neurovascular resilience is part of the target.

**Stack alert:** Because telmisartan is already in your stack, the useful question is not whether to chase novelty but whether BP, glucose, cognition, and recovery logs are moving in the same direction as the mechanism story.
